# ⚠️ Risk Valuation

## 🧠 What is Risk?

A risk is the possibility that something harmful may happen.

👉 In cybersecurity:
Risk = potential damage to systems, data, or operations

---

## 🎯 Why Risk Matters

Not all risks are equally important.

👉 Some require immediate action  
👉 Others can be monitored or ignored  

Organizations must prioritize based on risk level.

---

## 📊 Risk Value Formula

Risk can be estimated using:

Risk = Consequence × Likelihood

---

### 📌 Terms Explained

- Consequence → Impact or damage caused  
- Likelihood → Probability of occurrence  

---

## 🧪 Example (New)

### 🚗 Scenario: Losing Laptop

- Consequence → High (contains personal + work data)  
- Likelihood → Medium (possible but not frequent)  

👉 Risk = Medium–High → Needs protection (e.g., encryption)

---

## ⚖️ Risk Levels

| Risk Level | Meaning |
|-----------|--------|
| Low | Minor impact or rare |
| Medium | Manageable impact |
| High | Serious and urgent |

---

## 🧠 Special Case in Cybersecurity

Unlike real-world risks, cybersecurity risks are harder to measure.

👉 Why?
- Attackers constantly evolve  
- No fixed probability data  

---

## 📊 Likelihood in Cybersecurity

Instead of direct probability, likelihood depends on:

Likelihood = Adversary Capability × Adversary Motivation × Vulnerability Severity

---

## 🧩 Key Components

---

### 👤 1. Adversary (Attacker)

An entity trying to compromise a system.

---

### ⚙️ 2. Adversary Capability

- Skills  
- Tools  
- Resources  

👉 Example:
Advanced hackers → high capability  
Beginners → low capability  

---

### 🔥 3. Adversary Motivation

Why the attacker wants to attack:

- Money  
- Data  
- Political reasons  
- Curiosity  

---

### 🕳️ 4. Vulnerability

A weakness in the system.

👉 Example:
- Weak login system  
- Outdated software  

---

## 🧪 Example (New)

### 🏫 Scenario: School Portal Attack

- Capability → Medium (basic hacking tools available online)  
- Motivation → High (students want grade access)  
- Vulnerability → High (weak password system)  

👉 Likelihood = High  
👉 Risk = High → needs immediate fix  

---

## 🎯 Key Takeaway

Risk is not just about impact — it also depends on:

- Who is attacking  
- Why they are attacking  
- How easy the system is to break  

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

In cybersecurity, risk is dynamic and continuously changing.

Even a low-risk system today can become high-risk tomorrow if attacker motivation or capabilities increase.
